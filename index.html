Hi there!

I'm a high school sophomore from Oregon who loves learning about anything and everything. As a member of the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program, I've developed this website to help highlight some of my interests, projects, and achievements. In addition to international politics, cultural dialogue, creative writing, music, and social entrepreneurship, I love learning languages (focused on French, Korean, and Chinese right now), crocheting, drawing, and obsessing over anime and K-pop.

Writer

I've been scribbling down stories of unicorns and talking dogs since second grade, and creative writing remains one of my strongest passions and outlets in life. My work is focused on short stories, creative nonfiction, and young adult, but I'm also starting my first novel and love exploring different genres.

Aspiring Politician

I've always believed in the power of public policy and political leadership to change society for good. Through my involvement in Youth and Government, speech and debate, class council, and local political campaigns, I hope to set up a strong foundation for a career in diplomacy and/or domestic politics!

Flutist

I started playing flute in 5th grade band and haven't stopped since! In addition to school band, local orchestras, and honors ensembles, I enjoy performing in solo competitions and teaching private lessons.

Researcher

As a research intern in Dr. Margaret Burnett's lab at Oregon State University, I helped co-author several papers on how socioeconomic status, gender, and various other sociological factors can affect technology use. Through working with GenderMag, explainable AI, and human-computer interaction, we aim to reduce cognitive biases in existing software and make software intuitive and user-friendly for all people.
